Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

We practiced our skills on Visual Studio Code to create a Get to Know Me website. We learned how to encode photos and text within the website. By using Github we created a published website with its own domain to see the results of our work.

Logo A/B/C

Using Adobe Logo Maker and Canva Logo we created three logos that represented us as a person. I wanted to create possible business logos as well while doing this project. For Adobe, we put in text and picked an icon, and/or style we wanted. You could then move objects around, change colors, or change the text. These were the first three drafts.

UX Testing Logo Project

With the three logos drafted, we created a UX design study with three participants. I asked three types of questions: reflective, behavioral, and visceral. The answers we got from the participants helped us gather feedback about our logos. From my three participants' responses, I changed my logos drastically. I kept the elements that like enjoyed (color, font, spacing) but removed elements and replaced them. These were my final three logos that came from my UX design study.

Cartoon Portrait

Using layers for this cartoon portrait was vital. We used the eyedropper to color-match the shades on the photo we were cartooning. We then used the pen tool to mark out the spots we wanted to fill with said color. It was very easy to make mistakes throughout this process so much so I decided to scrap my progress multiple times. I found sometimes when the eyedropper wasn’t giving the color I wanted it was easy to change the shade to what you were looking for.

Good Design

By locating an example of a well-made design, we can dissect the elements to find components of good design. We used and learned how to use the arrange, frame, and text tools. We picked out elements within the design and applied them to design principles that we have learned throughout the class.

Short Form


Two-page Spread

For the magazine spread reproduction, I started by choosing a visually compelling two-page layout as my model. I created an 11x17” page in InDesign, using the color swatch and other tools to match the original design’s colors, fonts, and layout as closely as possible. I was able to ask AI to find a close match the font that was on the original. I used text fill to for it to write up some lorem ipsum words. The hardest part was finding an image without water marks or have it be blurry. I put the original inside of the one I created

Graphic Design Resume

In my design resume project, I focused on making a creative resume that would stand out for design roles. I tried out multiple different layouts or pieces to get ideas from and decided I needed to find one that I thought stuck out. I worked with InDesign’s text box controls, type-on-a-path, and text wrapping to create a unique layout that reflects my style while showcasing my skills and experience. I also created some not-real skills/jobs of things I do on a daily but don't actually do for work.

Advertisement

For the poster, I wrote about 50-100 words of engaging text and experimenting with layouts in InDesign. I focused on creating eye-catching visuals and strong typography to communicate the message effectively. I used the color palette tool and brought the poster to life.

Client Project

In the design consultation project, I collaborated with my manager from Lola’s Eatery to create merch designs for their upcoming launch. They gave me a few ideas and I created from there. I ended up making 5+ designs but I showed them the A/B samples for this project. I conducted a client meeting, developed a design brief, and presented A/B samples for feedback. After refining the design based on the client’s input, I delivered a final version that aligned with their goals. This project was a great way to step into the world of graphic design and getting paid to design.
